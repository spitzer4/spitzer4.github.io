Transit for Tourists

Making city navigation simple, intuitive, and tourist-friendly

Role
Lead UX/UI Designer
Duration
3 Months
Year
2025
01

The Challenge

How might we design a transit app that helps visitors navigate a new city with ease and confidence?

Most existing transit apps are designed with locals in mind — they assume you already know routes, stop names, or how to buy tickets. For first-time visitors, this creates friction: too many choices, unfamiliar terms, and stressful navigation experiences.

Tourists need an app that works the way they think: by landmarks, nearby stops, and clear, step-by-step directions.

02

The Solution

A visitor-first transit app that turns confusion into confidence

The redesigned experience puts travelers at the center. By combining landmark-based navigation, real-time updates, and a clear information hierarchy, the app makes public transit approachable, whether you’re traveling solo or with family.

Key Improvements

  • Streamlined Onboarding: Streamlined Onboarding: Minimal setup with options for solo travelers or families, plus offline language support.
  • Visitor-Friendly Navigation: Search by landmark (“Art Institute of Chicago”) instead of stop name; step-by-step directions resemble a guided map.
  • Nearby Transit View: One-tap access to all buses and trains nearby — no need to know routes in advance.
  • Ticket Integration: Tickets and passes are always a single tap away.
  • Bold Visual System: High-contrast colors and clear typography for use outdoors and on the go.
03

Research

Working within stakeholder constraints, I focused on actionable research methods that could quickly inform design decisions.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed 3 direct competitors (local chambers of commerce) and 2 indirect competitors (community organizations and local tourism sites) to understand:

  • Common navigation patterns and content organization
  • How other sites handle event promotion (like farmers markets)
  • Visual trends that signal trustworthiness in civic organizations

Key Insights

Most chamber websites fell into two problematic categories—either overly corporate and intimidating for small businesses, or visually outdated with poor information architecture.

Critical Success Patterns

  • Event visibility drives engagement
  • Visual balance matters
  • Navigation simplicity wins

No existing chamber site successfully combined approachable community feel with structured, accessible information architecture - creating a clear opportunity for a solution that serves both small businesses and community members effectively.

User Persona

Based on stakeholder input and community demographics, I developed a primary persona to represent a typical Chamber website visitor: small business owners looking for networking opportunities, resources, and community connection.

04

Design Process

I approached the design with clear objectives while remaining flexible about implementation realities.

Design Principles

  • Information accessibility first: Make finding farmers market details effortless.
  • Modern but approachable: Update the visual presence without alienating existing community members.
  • Stakeholder-aligned: Work within organizational priorities and technical limitations

The Reality of Implementation

My initial mockups included a custom header design that would better showcase the farmers market and a footer with enhanced community links. However, during implementation, several factors influenced the final design:

Stakeholder Feedback

  • Custom images/videos dictated layout on Farmers Market homepage
  • Awards section reordered to highlight most recent first
  • Navigation link to SSA site required
  • Logo choice dictated header text (removed duplicate text)

Wix Platform Constraints

  • Header layout limited by Wix editor
  • Event section used Wix's preset layout → limited customization
  • Some decorative elements removed due to spacing issues

Rather than compromise the core user experience, I adapted the design to work within these constraints while maintaining the project's key objectives. This meant finding creative solutions within Wix's framework and advocating for the most critical user experience elements.

Results

Despite implementation challenges, the redesigned website successfully achieves its primary goals:

  • Farmers market information is now prominently featured and easily accessible
  • The overall visual presentation feels contemporary and professional
  • Community members can quickly find the information they need

What I Learned

Designing within constraints isn't limitation—it's problem-solving

This project reinforced that successful design often happens within real-world limitations. Learning to adapt while protecting core user needs is a crucial skill.

Stakeholder communication is design work too

Managing expectations and explaining design decisions became as important as the visual design itself.

Platform limitations spark creativity

Working within Wix's constraints pushed me to find innovative solutions I might not have considered with unlimited technical freedom.
