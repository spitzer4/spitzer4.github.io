Simplifying Dashboard Onboarding in Power BI

Adding a guided, step-by-step workflow to reduce the steep learning curve in Power BI.

Role
UX/UI Designer
Duration
1 week
Year
2025
01

The Challenge

Business intelligence (BI) platforms like Power BI are powerful but intimidating. New users often face:

  • A steep learning curve due to technical jargon.
  • Overwhelming UIs with too many options at once.
  • Lack of guidance when building their first dashboard, leading to frustration and low adoption.

I set out to design a guided workflow - a structured, supportive process that helps new users build their first dashboard with confidence.

How might we design a guided onboarding workflow that helps users build their first dashboard with confidence and clarity?

02

The Solution

I designed a guided workflow that walks users step-by-step through creating a first dashboard, combining familiar onboarding patterns with new differentiators.

Key Features:

  • Goal-first onboarding: Starts with “What question are you trying to answer?” to map users to relevant templates.
  • Persona-driven templates: Marketers, analysts, executives see tailored templates and vocabulary.
  • Explainable recommendations: Suggested chart types with human-friendly reasoning.
  • Celebratory milestone: completion screen + next steps to improve retention.
03

Research

Learning from the Leaders: Best Practices and Pitfalls

I analyzed onboarding flows in leading BI tools to identify best practices and gaps.

  • Tableau: fast “Show Me” chart suggestions, but jargon is intimidating.
  • Looker Studio: free + templates, but generic and require tuning.
  • Power BI: tutorials exist, but overwhelming UI and steep learning curve.

Patterns worth reusing:

  • Sample data + templates
  • Step-by-step tutorials
  • Smart chart suggestions

Gaps & opportunities:

  • Contextual onboarding tied to business questions
  • Persona-driven flows
  • Explainable chart suggestions
  • Celebrating milestones

Balancing Speed, Trust, and Context

Based on user research, I developed two primary personas:

  • Maya (Marketing Manager): Wants quick, contextual dashboards tied to campaign goals.
  • Jordan (Data Analyst): Wants transparency, efficiency, and control over data prep.

What users say:

Insight: Current flows optimize for speed, but fail at context and trust. The design needs to balance both.

04

Design Process

  1. Mapped common onboarding flows from BI tools.
  2. Defined personas and goals to guide feature priorities.
  3. Sketched flows around a 4-step journey: Pick template → Connect data → Customize → Publish.
  4. Prototyped in Figma, testing layouts for clarity and reducing jargon.
  5. Iterated with feedback by evaluating against success criteria (time-to-first-dashboard, trust/confidence).
05

Results

While this was a conceptual redesign, I defined clear success metrics:

  • Time to first dashboard: Under 10 minutes with sample data.
  • Completion rate: Higher onboarding retention through guided steps.
  • Confidence: Users report understanding why charts/templates were chosen.
  • Adoption: More dashboards published/shared in first session.

Impact: This workflow reduces the steep learning curve of Power BI, making analytics more approachable for non-technical users while still respecting the needs of analysts.
