Logan Square Chamber of Commerce

Redesigning Connection, Locally

My Role: Product Designer
Timeline: March 2025 – May 2025

A full website redesign for the Logan Square Chamber of Commerce, aimed at modernizing its digital presence, improving user experience for small business owners and residents, and establishing a more cohesive brand identity.

PROBLEM

An Outdated, Fragmented Digital Experience

The Logan Square Chamber of Commerce's website was failing to meet the needs of its two primary audiences—local small business owners and community members. The site suffered from poor navigation, lack of mobile responsiveness, and an inconsistent brand identity. Information about events, membership benefits, and business resources was buried or incomplete. This disconnect undermined the Chamber’s mission of promoting local commerce and fostering community engagement.

SOLUTION

A Brand-Driven, Community-Focused Redesign

I led a full website redesign with a focus on aligning the digital experience with the Chamber’s values and brand identity. Through user interviews, analytics, and competitor research, I identified key audience needs and pain points. This informed a clear site architecture built around five core sections: About, Membership, Community, Events, and Farmers Market. Key design decisions included:

  • Mobile-first UX to improve usability for all demographics
  • Clear CTAs guiding users to take action (join, attend, explore)
  • New visual identity including a logo, color palette, typography, and brand guidelines
  • Custom branding elements rooted in the Logan Square neighborhood identity
  • Event calendar and vendor listings to boost engagement and transparency
This project demonstrates my ability to translate brand values into digital experiences, as well as to design for consumer-facing products that drive real engagement.

IMPACT

A Cohesive Brand and Usable Website That Serves the Community

The new website not only looks modern and cohesive but also makes it easier for small business owners to find resources and for residents to engage with their local community. The updated brand system is now used in digital and print marketing, establishing a stronger and more consistent Chamber presence. Key outcomes:

  • Increased clarity and usability across all devices
  • A revitalized brand that reflects community pride
  • A flexible system that can scale with future Chamber needs
