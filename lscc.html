Logan Square Chamber of Commerce

Redesigning Connection, Locally

My Role: Product Designer
Timeline: March 2025 - May 2025

A full website redesign for the Logan Square Chamber of Commerce, aimed at modernizing its digital presence, improving usability for small business owners and residents, and creating a consistent, scalable brand identity.
This project showcases my ability to lead a product from discovery through delivery—researching user needs, building a strong site architecture, and crafting a brand-driven digital experience that supports real community engagement.

PROBLEM

An Outdated, Fragmented Digital Experience

The Chamber's original website failed to serve its two key audiences—local business owners and residents. Key issues included:

  • Outdated design and inconsistent branding
  • Poor navigation and overwhelming page layouts
  • No mobile optimization
  • Key info (event listings, membership benefits) was buried or outdated
The result? Missed opportunities to promote local businesses, reduced event engagement, and a digital presence that didn’t reflect the vibrancy of the Logan Square community.

RESEARCH & DISCOVERY

Understanding the Community and Its Needs

I began with qualitative and quantitative research to uncover core user pain points and goals.

  • Stakeholder interviews with Chamber staff highlighted content maintenance and branding issues
  • Google Analytics review showed high mobile traffic but low engagement on mobile pages
  • Competitor analysis revealed cleaner navigation systems and clearer CTAs

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Building a More Intuitive Structure

I developed a new site map organized around five clear sections:

  1. About
  2. Membership
  3. Community
  4. Events
  5. Farmers Market
This structure reflected actual user goals and aligned with the Chamber's priorities.

WIREFRAMES

Testing Structure Before Style

I created wireframes for key pages in both desktop and mobile formats to validate layout decisions and prioritize clarity across breakpoints.

UI DESIGN & INTERACTION

Turning Values into Actionable Interfaces

Final high-fidelity designs emphasized approachability, community, and action.

  • Mobile-first layouts to match how most users access the site
  • Mobile-first layouts to match how most users access the site
  • Clear calls to action for joining the Chamber, attending events, or exploring vendors
  • Interactive event calendar and vendor listings to support transparency and engagement
IMPACT

A Usable, Brand-Aligned Website That Works for the Community

The final site now serves as a flexible, scalable tool for promoting local commerce and fostering engagement—whether residents are browsing events on their phones or business owners are applying for membership.

Key outcomes:

  • Increased mobile usability and event discoverability
  • A brand system now used across Chamber email templates, posters, and signage
  • Positive feedback from Chamber staff and early users
REFLECTION

What I Learned

This project helped me hone my ability to balance user needs, stakeholder priorities, and brand expression. If I could go back, I’d also conduct a post-launch usability study to capture more qualitative feedback from residents and vendors.

Next up? I'm excited to apply these learnings to other civic, nonprofit, or community-driven design challenges.
