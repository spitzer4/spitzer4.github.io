Project Overview

The Logan Square Chamber of Commerce needed a complete digital transformation to better serve local businesses and residents. The existing website was outdated, difficult to navigate, and failed to showcase the vibrant community spirit that defines Logan Square.

This project involved comprehensive UX research, brand refresh, and full website redesign with a focus on improving user experience for both business owners seeking resources and residents looking to connect with their local community.

Logan Square Chamber Website - Before & After Comparison

The Challenge

The Logan Square Chamber of Commerce faced several critical issues with their digital presence:

🔍

Poor Information Architecture

Users struggled to find basic information about membership, events, and local business resources.

📱

Mobile Responsiveness

The site wasn't optimized for mobile devices, limiting accessibility for users on-the-go.

🎨

Brand Inconsistency

Visual identity lacked cohesion and failed to reflect the dynamic nature of Logan Square.

Competitive Analysis

To better understand the landscape, I conducted a competitive audit of three direct competitors—Chicago Chamber of Commerce, Wicker Park Bucktown Chamber, and Lincoln Park Chamber—and two indirect competitors—Choose Chicago and LoganSquarist.

I evaluated each on first impressions, usability, features, visual design, accessibility, and content strategy. This analysis helped identify opportunities to differentiate the Logan Square Chamber site and bring it in line with modern expectations.

Key Takeaways

✨

Need for Visual Refresh

While competitors like Choose Chicago use dynamic visuals and bold branding, the Logan Square Chamber site felt dated and underutilized imagery to convey neighborhood energy.

📱

Mobile Optimization Gaps

Direct competitors offered better mobile UX, though some had clunky menus. Improving mobile usability was a clear opportunity.

🔍

Limited Interactive Features

Most chambers offered business directories and event calendars, but lacked deeper tools like interactive maps, personalized recommendations, or filterable content.

♿

Accessibility Opportunities

Few competitors had strong accessibility practices (alt text, keyboard navigation). Building accessibility from the start would be a differentiator.

🧭

Simpler User Flows

Content on many competitor sites was buried behind dense menus. Clearer calls-to-action and streamlined navigation were top priorities for redesign.

Results & Impact

The redesigned website just launched, so metrics aren’t available yet—but the Chamber team shared positive early feedback, especially about easier content updates, clearer navigation, and brand alignment.

Key Learnings

This project reinforced the importance of community-centered design and stakeholder collaboration. Working directly with local business owners and residents provided invaluable insights that shaped every design decision.

The success of this redesign demonstrates how thoughtful UX design can strengthen community connections and drive real business impact for local organizations.
