Focus

Problem

An Outdated Digital Gateway & Hidden Information

The existing Logan Square Chamber of Commerce website suffered from two primary issues:

  1. Aesthetics & Credibility: The site felt visually outdated and cluttered, undermining the Chamber's professional image and vital role in the community.
  2. Information Architecture: Key community information, especially details about the widely popular Farmers Market, was buried, making it difficult for visitors and tourists to find essential details like operating hours, vendor lists, and location.

Solution

A Modern, Mobile-First Design Focused on Accessibility

I designed a responsive, modern website that prioritized two main goals: a refreshed aesthetic and clear, concise navigation. The core of the solution was creating a dedicated, information-rich, and easy-to-navigate section for the Farmers Market.

Features high-impact photography and immediately surfaces key details (dates, times, location) to solve the "hidden information" problem.

Focuses on responsiveness, ensuring core calls-to-action and primary navigation are immediately accessible on a small screen to address the mobile-unfriendly nature of the old site.

Main nav/header

Cleans up the navigation to a streamlined list, making it easier for users to jump to sections like "Farmers Market," "Membership," and "Community Events."

Research

Understanding the User and the Landscape

Main Insight: Successful Chamber websites prioritize large, clean photography and a simple, task-oriented navigation. They treat their Farmers Market as a primary product, giving it high visibility. This confirmed the need to give the market its own clear, top-level navigation link.

Setbacks

Navigating Stakeholder Vision and Technical Limitations

This project's real challenge was balancing an ideal user experience with real-world constraints.

Stakeholder Constraints: Refining Content & Visuals

  • The initial structure of the Farmers Market page had to be adapted to prominently showcase specific videos and images requested by the Chamber, leading to a shift in the content hierarchy.
  • The Awards section for the Market was updated to be anti-chronological, ensuring the most relevant and recent achievements are immediately visible to users.
  • A new, mandatory SSA Website nav link was required, slightly altering the primary navigation flow.
  • Due to the stakeholders' chosen logo already containing the full text "Logan Square Chamber of Commerce," the redundant text label in the header was removed, streamlining the masthead.

Wix Constraints: The Reality of Platform Design

  • Header and Footer formatting in Wix was inflexible, forcing a simpler design than the original mockup. This limited sophisticated alignment and spacing, resulting in a cleaner, but less custom, look.
  • The display of Member and Community Events was largely dictated by Wix's default event layout, preventing the detailed custom layouts planned in Figma.
  • Difficulties maintaining consistent, pixel-perfect spacing and alignment in the Wix editor led to the strategic removal of decorative elements to ensure a cleaner, more stable, and professional appearance across all devices.

Reflection

The Art of Design in Reality

This project was a valuable lesson in the essential skill of translating stakeholder intent and business goals into a final product under strict technical and resource constraints.

While the final design differs from the initial, ideal mockups, the deployed website is significantly more modern, accessible, and user-friendly than the previous version. The core goals - making the site feel less outdated and ensuring the Farmers Market information is easy to find - were successfully met. The need to adapt the design based on Wix's limitations and stakeholder feedback proved that effective UX is not just about creating beautiful mockups, but about delivering a functional, impactful solution that meets the real-world needs of the client and the user.
