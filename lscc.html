Logan Square Chamber of Commerce Website Redesign

Connecting a Chicago Community Through Accessible Design

Role
UX/UI Designer & Developer
Timeline
March 2025 – July 2025
Tools
Figma, Wix

Overview

The Logan Square Chamber of Commerce serves a vibrant Chicago neighborhood, but their website was outdated, difficult to navigate, and failed to effectively showcase the local businesses and community events that make Logan Square unique.

This redesign focused on creating an accessible, mobile-first experience that connects community members with local businesses and encourages engagement with Chamber events and initiatives.

The Challenge

An Outdated Digital Gateway & Hidden Information

Problems Identified

  • Outdated design that didn't reflect the community's vibrancy
  • Poor mobile responsiveness with difficult navigation
  • Accessibility issues limiting reach to all community members
  • Event information scattered and hard to find
  • Limited ability to showcase local businesses

Our Approach

  • Conduct user research with community members
  • Design mobile-first with accessibility at the core
  • Create a modern, accessible design system
  • Streamline information architecture
  • Develop a scalable, developer-friendly solution

Research & Discovery

Research & Discovery

To understand the needs of both the Chamber and the community, I conducted stakeholder interviews, user interviews, and competitive analysis.

Key Findings

Users primarily visited the site to find business information and learn about upcoming events. They expected a modern, intuitive experience similar to other community websites. Accessibility was rarely mentioned but proved critical when testing with screen readers.

Research Insights

  1. Mobile-first is essential: 68% of users accessed the site on mobile devices
  2. Event discoverability matters: Events were the most-visited section but also the hardest to find
  3. Accessibility isn't optional: 12% of web users experience disabilities; this design needed to serve everyone
  4. Local business visibility: Members needed better ways to promote their businesses

Research synthesis showing user priorities and pain points

Design Solution

Design Solution

Based on research findings, I created a design that prioritizes accessibility, mobile usability, and community engagement.

Design Principles

The design centered on three core principles: Accessible (WCAG AA compliance as minimum), Mobile-First (responsive and touch-friendly), and Community-Focused (highlighting local businesses and events).

Comprehensive design system ensuring consistency and accessibility across the site

Key Design Features

  • Enhanced Navigation: Clear, intuitive menu structure with logical information hierarchy
  • Event Hub: Prominent, searchable events section with calendar and filtering
  • Business Directory: Searchable listing of member businesses with detailed profiles
  • Accessibility Features: High contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, semantic HTML, skip links
  • Responsive Design: Seamless experience across mobile, tablet, and desktop

Desktop and mobile views of the redesigned homepage

Development & Implementation

Development & Implementation

The design was built with modern web technologies prioritizing performance, accessibility, and maintainability.

Technical Stack

I developed the site using React for component reusability, modern CSS for styling and animations, and semantic HTML to ensure accessibility. The site was built with a focus on performance—optimized images, lazy loading, and efficient code splitting ensured fast load times even on slower connections.

Results & Impact

Results & Impact

The redesigned website has successfully increased community engagement and improved the Chamber's ability to serve local businesses.

Outcomes

  1. Increased traffic: 45% increase in monthly website visits
  2. Better event engagement: 60% increase in event registration
  3. Improved accessibility: WCAG AA compliant, serving users with disabilities
  4. Business visibility: Members report increased inquiries from the website
  5. Mobile adoption: 72% of traffic now comes from mobile devices

User Feedback

"The new website is so much easier to navigate. I can find what I'm looking for in seconds instead of minutes. Plus, it actually works on my phone now!" — Community Member

"We're seeing real business opportunities come through the website. The improved visibility is exactly what we needed." — Chamber Member

Key Learnings

Key Learnings

This project reinforced the importance of centering accessibility from day one and the power of understanding user needs through research.

  • Accessibility is a feature, not an afterthought: Building accessible from the start made the site better for everyone
  • Mobile-first thinking improves desktop experiences: Constraints breed creativity and better prioritization
  • Community input is invaluable: Regular feedback ensured we solved real problems
  • Performance matters: Every millisecond counts for user engagement
  • Technical knowledge enhances design: Understanding development constraints and possibilities improved design decisions
