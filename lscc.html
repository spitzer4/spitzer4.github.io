Logan Square Chamber of Commerce

Redesigning Connection, Locally

My Role: Product Designer
Timeline: March 2025 – May 2025

A full website redesign for the Logan Square Chamber of Commerce, aimed at modernizing its digital presence, improving user experience for small business owners and residents, and establishing a more cohesive brand identity.

Problem

The existing Chamber of Commerce website was outdated, difficult to navigate, and lacked a clear visual identity. It failed to effectively communicate the value of Chamber membership or highlight local businesses and events. The site needed to better serve its two main audiences: local business owners and community members seeking resources and events.

Process

  1. Empathize
  2. Define
  3. Ideate
  4. Prototype
  5. Test

1. Empathize

Goal: Understand the users, their pain points, and their motivations around sustainable shopping.

User Personas

Methods

Interviews with a few small business owners, informal feedback from community members, and review of current site analytics.

Competitive Analysis

Researched direct and indirect competitors, noting:

  • Clear calls-to-action
  • Modern, mobile-friendly design
  • Event calendars and local business directories

2. Define

Goal: Synthesize research into clear insights and define the core user problem.

Key Insights & User Needs

  • Users need to find information quickly - current site structure is confusing.
  • The website should communicate credibility and community value.
  • Local businesses want visibility; residents want to engage with events.
  • The brand lacked consistency - no visual system or tone of voice.
How might we create a modern, accessible website that connects local businesses and residents while reinforcing the Chamber's brand and community presence?

3. Ideate

Goal: Generate creative ideas to solve the defined user problem.

Solutions

  • Simplified site architecture with 5 core sections: About, Membership, Community, Events, and Farmers Market
  • Prominent CTAs throughout
  • Visual language inspired by the town's identity
  • Event calendar

Branding

  • Developed a logo concept, color palette, and typography system
  • Created brand guidelines for future marketing materials

4. Prototype

Goal: Build a visual representation of the app.

5. Test

Goal: Gather feedback and iterate on the prototype based on user input.
