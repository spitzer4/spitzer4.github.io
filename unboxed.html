UnBoxed

Simplified Sustainable Shopping

Solo Project
My Role: Product Designer
Timeline: March 2025 – April 2025

A zero waste shopping app aimed at simplifying the eco-friendly shopping experience for environmentally conscious users. The app aims to bridge the gap between intent and action for those looking to reduce their environmental footprint.

Problem

As sustainability becomes a growing priority, consumers are increasingly seeking ways to reduce waste—but navigating the eco-friendly shopping landscape can be confusing and overwhelming. There's a clear need for a centralized, user-friendly platform that empowers eco-conscious users to shop sustainably with confidence and ease.

Process

  1. Empathize
  2. Define
  3. Ideate
  4. Prototype
  5. Test

1. Empathize

Goal: Understand the users, their pain points, and their motivations around sustainable shopping.

User Personas

  • Sarah Miller - The Eco-Conscious Millennial
    Values: Deeply committed to sustainability, wants stylish, high-quality zero-waste products.
    Pain Points: Difficulty sourcing truly sustainable items, greenwashing, and time-consuming research.
    Needs: Trustworthy, convenient platform that aligns with her values and aesthetics.
  • David Chen - The Busy Parent Seeking Convenience
    Values: Sustainability for the whole family without added stress.
    Pain Points: Time constraints, inconvenience, and lack of accessible kid-friendly sustainable products.
    Needs: A quick, easy, and reliable way to shop eco-friendly essentials in one place.
  • Emily Carter - The Conscious Beginner
    Values: Learning and growing into sustainable habits; budget-conscious.
    Pain Points: Overwhelmed by info, unsure where to begin, and worried about costs.
    Needs: Clear guidance, starter-friendly options, and affordable alternatives.

2. Define

Goal: Synthesize research into clear insights and define the core user problem.

Key Insights

  • Trust is critical
  • Time and mental load are barriers
  • Affordability and accessibility matter
  • Aesthetic + function = adoption
  • Eco-curious beginners also need loving
  • Community and inspiration are critical

Defined User Problem

Users looking to live more sustainably struggle to find trustworthy, affordable, and convenient eco-friendly products due to confusing information, greenwashing, and lack of beginner guidance – making it hard to shift to sustainable or consistent habits.

3. Ideate

Goal: Generate creative ideas to solve the defined user problem.

4. Prototype

Goal: Build a visual representation of the app.

5. Test
