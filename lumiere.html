Lumiere

This case study details the design and development of a static website for Lumiere Fragrance, a fictional luxury fragrance brand. The project aimed to create a visually captivating and user-friendly online experience that would showcase the brand's signature scents, embody its commitment to quality and craftsmanship, and ultimately drive customer engagement. My role encompassed both UX/UI design and front-end web development, allowing for a cohesive and integrated approach.

Project Goals

  • Develop a visually stunning website that reflects the brand's luxurious image.
  • Create a user-friendly experience for browsing signature scents.
  • Effectively communicate the brand's story and values.
  • Implement a responsive design that adapts to various screen sizes.
  • Deliver a clean and well-documented codebase for future maintenance and updates.

UX/UI Design Process

User Research and Iteration

The design of the Lumiere Fragrance website was an iterative process, informed by user feedback and testing. Several key changes were implemented between versions to improve user engagement and overall experience.

Version 1.0 to 2.0

The initial version of the website (v1.0) served as a foundation for testing and gathering feedback. Based on initial user reviews and heuristic evaluations, the following changes were made for version 2.0:

  • Improved Readability: The "About" section text was broken into smaller paragraphs to enhance readability and make the content more digestible.
  • Enhanced Social Engagement: The size of social media links was increased to make them more prominent and encourage social sharing.
  • Engaging Newsletter Signup: The newsletter signup title was changed to "Get Exclusive Access to New Scents & Special Offers" to be more compelling and benefit-driven.
  • Stronger Call to Action: The primary call to action was updated from "Discover Scents" to "Find Your Scent" to be more action-oriented and personalized.

Version 2.0 to 3.0

Building on the improvements in v2.0, further refinements were made for version 3.0 based on continued user feedback and analysis:

  • Enhanced About Section: Key words in the "About" section were bolded to draw attention to important information and improve scannability.
  • Enriched Scent Descriptions: Detailed descriptions were added to each fragrance to provide users with more information and help them make informed choices.
  • Refined Call to Action: The call to action was further refined to "Discover Your Signature Scent" to be even more personalized and aspirational.

Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes were created to outline the website's layout and structure, focusing on usability and information hierarchy. These wireframes were then translated into high-fidelity mockups, showcasing the visual design, typography, and color palette.

Style Guide

The visual design was carefully crafted to reflect Lumiere's brand identity. A sophisticated color palette, elegant typography, and high-quality imagery were selected to create a sense of luxury and exclusivity.

Color Palette

The color palette plays a crucial role in conveying the brand's identity. We chose a three-color scheme centered around a rich gold (#D4AF37) as the primary accent. Gold is universally associated with luxury, opulence, and prestige, reflecting the high quality and exclusivity of Lumiere's fragrances. It evokes a sense of timelessness and sophistication, aligning with the brand's desire to create fragrances that transcend fleeting trends.

To balance the richness of the gold, we incorporated a dark gray (#2C2C2C) as the secondary color. This dark gray provides a sophisticated and understated backdrop, allowing the gold to truly shine. It also offers excellent contrast for text, ensuring readability and a user-friendly experience. The dark gray adds a touch of modernity and refinement, complementing the classic elegance of the gold.

Finally, an off-white or cream (#F8F8F8) was selected as the background color. This soft, neutral tone creates a clean and elegant canvas, allowing the fragrances and other visual elements to stand out. It provides a subtle warmth and avoids the starkness of pure white, contributing to a more inviting and luxurious feel. The off-white background also enhances the overall sense of sophistication and refinement.

Typography

The typography choices were equally important in establishing the brand's visual identity. For headings and titles, we selected Playfair Display and Lora. Playfair Display is a classic serif font known for its elegant and refined letterforms. It exudes a sense of timelessness and sophistication, echoing the brand's commitment to creating fragrances that are both classic and contemporary. The use of a serif font for headings adds a touch of formality and grandeur, reflecting the luxurious nature of the brand.

For body text and smaller text elements, we opted for Poppins. Poppins is a clean, modern, and highly legible sans-serif font. It provides excellent readability and ensures a user-friendly experience, particularly for longer text blocks like fragrance descriptions or about us content. The use of a sans-serif font for body text creates a subtle contrast with the serif font used for headings, adding visual interest while maintaining a sense of balance and harmony.

The combination of Playfair Display, Lora, and Poppins creates a visually compelling and harmonious typographic system. It balances elegance and readability, reflecting the brand's commitment to both aesthetics and user experience. The typography reinforces the overall sense of luxury and sophistication, contributing to a cohesive and impactful brand identity for Lumiere Fragrance.

Web Development

The website was built using HTML for structure and CSS for styling, adhering to best practices for clean code and maintainability.

  • HTML Structure: Semantic HTML5 elements were used to create a clear and logical structure for the website's content, improving accessibility and SEO.
  • CSS Styling: CSS was used to style all aspects of the website's appearance, including typography, colors, layout, and responsiveness. Flexbox was utilized for flexible and efficient layout design. A mobile-first approach was considered to ensure optimal viewing on smaller screens.
  • Image Optimization: Images were optimized for web use to reduce file sizes without sacrificing quality, improving page load times.

Design & Development Integration

The UX/UI design and web development phases were closely integrated. The design decisions made during the UX phase directly informed the development process. For example, the elegant typography and color palette were implemented using CSS, and the layout defined in the wireframes was brought to life using HTML and Flexbox.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Creating a visually engaging scent slider with only HTML and CSS.
  • Solution: Leveraged CSS properties like overflow-x: auto and scroll-snap to create a horizontally scrolling container for the scent cards. While basic, this provides the desired functionality without JavaScript.

Results

  • A visually appealing and user-friendly website that effectively showcases Lumiere Fragrance's brand and products.
  • A clean and well-structured codebase that is easy to maintain and update.
  • A functional scent showcase using only HTML and CSS.

Conclusion

This project successfully demonstrated the ability to create a professional-looking website using only HTML and CSS. While the absence of JavaScript and media queries presented some limitations in terms of advanced interactivity and fully responsive design, the project achieved its core goals of visual appeal, user-friendliness, and effective brand communication. This case study highlights the importance of semantic HTML, well-structured CSS, careful planning, and the synergy between UX/UI design and web development in creating a successful online presence. For future iterations, incorporating JavaScript and media queries would significantly enhance the site's functionality and responsiveness.
